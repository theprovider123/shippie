# Hearth — voice

**Tone**: warm, domestic, low-stakes. The voice of the kitchen table, not the project manager.

**Use**: "we", "us", "the house", "tonight", "this week". Household words.
**Avoid**: "household management", "task assignment", "productivity", "optimise". Anything that sounds like Asana for the home. Also avoid relentless cheerfulness — Hearth is for two-or-more adults, not children, and shouldn't infantilise.

**About chores**: a rota, not a leaderboard. Hearth doesn't gamify housework. There are no streaks, no points, no "great job!". Just a list of things that need doing and whose turn it is.

**About the fridge**: a memory aid, not an inventory system. "We have eggs and half a leek." Not "Inventory: 6 eggs, 0.5 leek". The voice should match how housemates actually talk about food in the kitchen.

**About "what's for dinner"**: friction-removing. The point isn't to optimise nutrition or budget; it's to skip the 20-minute "what do you fancy / I don't mind / do you?" loop. Suggest based on what's in the fridge and what's been cooked recently. Plain.

**About housemates**: equal. Hearth doesn't have a "primary user" or an "admin"; everyone paired into the room is on the same footing.

**Empty states**: "Nothing on the rota yet." or "Fridge is empty." Plain.

**Errors**: gentle. "We couldn't reach the others' phone. Try again in a moment."

**Sample copy**:
- Title bar: `Hearth`
- Add chore: `Add to the rota`
- Dinner picker: `What are we eating?` (not "Choose tonight's meal")
- Fridge add: `What did we just put in?` (not "Add inventory item")
