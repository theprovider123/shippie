# Symptom Diary — voice

**Tone**: clinical-but-kind. The voice of a respected specialist nurse — careful, accurate, never patronising. Patterns, not predictions.

**Use**: "symptom", "trigger", "medication", "flare", "log", "doctor". Medical-adjacent words used plainly.
**Avoid**: "your wellness journey", "manage your condition", "take control", "fight back". Anything that sounds like a Big Pharma ad campaign. Also avoid pseudo-medical authority — Symptom Diary doesn't diagnose, doesn't recommend, doesn't say "this looks like…".

**About observation**: the app's job is to record honestly so the doctor can see the pattern. "Headache 4/5, after low sleep, after wine, day 12 of cycle." Not "Mild headache (it'll pass!)". The user knows what they felt; the app records it.

**About patterns**: shown but not interpreted. A weekly chart of symptom intensity. The user and their doctor decide what the pattern means; the app doesn't say "your symptoms correlate with stress" because that's not what an app should claim.

**About export**: this is half the product. The PDF export should look like something a specialist's secretary would file. Title, date range, log table, symptom-frequency chart, medication timeline. Print-ready. The doctor visit is where the value lands.

**About chronic illness**: takes it seriously. People with autoimmune, IBS, fibromyalgia, POTS, long COVID have already been talked-down-to by half a dozen apps. Symptom Diary doesn't add to that pile.

**Empty states**: "No entries yet." Plain.

**Errors**: factual. "Couldn't generate the PDF — not enough entries in this range. Add at least one log to export."

**Sample copy**:
- Title bar: `Symptom Diary`
- Log action: `Log symptom` / `Log medication`
- Trigger field: `What might have triggered this?` (not "Your trigger today")
- Export action: `Export for doctor`
- Privacy note: `Symptom Diary stays on this phone. The PDF you generate is yours to share — or not.`
