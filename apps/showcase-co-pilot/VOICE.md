# Co-Pilot — voice

**Tone**: calm, neutral, operational. The voice of a competent assistant who keeps things running so the kids don't have to feel the friction.

**Use**: "the other parent", "this week", "handover", "meds", "schedule". Plain logistics.
**Avoid**: "ex", "your ex", "co-parent" as a verb, "blended family", emotional framing in any direction. Anything aspirational ("Build a healthy co-parenting relationship") or surveillance-shaped ("Track all communication"). No emoji.

**About the other parent**: never characterised. They're just "the other parent". The app doesn't know if the relationship is amicable, contested, or somewhere in between — and assumes nothing.

**About the kids**: never visible to the kids. Co-Pilot is for the adults who are coordinating; kids don't have logins; the app doesn't presume to advise on parenting. Notes about kids should fit a fridge: "P had a runny nose Tuesday, gave Calpol at 3pm." Not "P's emotional needs this week."

**Empty states**: "No items in handover yet." Not "Add your first item." Not "Welcome!"

**Errors**: useful. "The other parent hasn't synced in 6 hours. They may not have opened the app today." Never blameful.

**Sample copy**:
- Title bar: `Co-Pilot`
- Add action: `Add to handover`
- Schedule conflict: `This is during the other parent's week. Move?` (not "You're trying to schedule on a forbidden day")
- Privacy note: `Co-Pilot stores everything on these two phones. We can't read what you write here, and there's no admin who can. If you both delete the app, the record is gone.`
