# Cycle — voice

**Tone**: matter-of-fact. Body-literacy, not femcare. The voice of an old friend who's a midwife — practical, unflinching, doesn't moralise.

**Use**: "period", "cycle", "ovulation", "bleeding", "cramps". The medical words. "Day 14", not "Day 14 of your beautiful cycle".
**Avoid**: "your special day", "feminine wellness", "monthly visitor", "Aunt Flo", flowers, butterflies, anything pink-and-cute. Also avoid "track your goddess energy" tone from the manifesting end. Also avoid medical-jargon overload — find the middle.

**About prediction**: honest. "Predicted next period in 4–6 days based on the last 6 cycles." Always with the range; never a single number masquerading as certainty. Cycles vary; the app is a tool, not an oracle.

**About fertility**: factual. The app shows the fertile window. It doesn't say "you're trying to conceive" or "this is a safe day". It assumes the user knows their own situation better than the app does.

**About privacy**: explicit and structural. Not a marketing line — a UI surface. "These records exist on this phone only. Cycle has no servers. If you choose to share with a partner, only the two of you have the keys." This is the load-bearing claim post-Roe, and it has to be true and visible.

**About a partner**: optional. Solo is the default. If a partner is paired, what they see is whatever the user explicitly chose to share — usually just "she's on day 3" or "fertile window opens Tuesday", not the full record.

**Empty states**: "No cycles logged yet." Plain.

**Errors**: structural. "Couldn't reach the relay. Your data is safe — it's only on this phone. Reconnect when you can."

**Sample copy**:
- Title bar: `Cycle`
- Log action: `Log today`
- Symptom add: `Add cramping`, `Add bleeding`, `Add mood`
- Privacy banner (settings): `Cycle stores on this phone. Nothing leaves unless you share it.`
