# Ledger — voice

**Tone**: sober, accurate. Spreadsheet voice. The accountant's voice, not the financial coach's.

**Use**: "spent", "received", "category", "amount", "month", "year", "export". Plain bookkeeping.
**Avoid**: "your financial journey", "reach your goals", "wealth", "freedom", "save smarter". Anything from the personal-finance influencer ecosystem. Also avoid emojis, motivational quotes, streak counters, "you're doing great!".

**About tracking**: Ledger records what happened. It does not advise. It does not tell the user they spent too much on coffee this month. It does not predict next month's spending. It does not suggest categories. It just records and aggregates.

**About categories**: the user picks them. There's no opinionated default chart of accounts. If the user wants a category called "stupid stuff" they can have one.

**About export**: respected. CSV is the export. The user owns the data. There's no premium tier that gates the export. The export is the product as much as the input form.

**About privacy**: factual. "No bank connections. No Plaid. No aggregator. Numbers live on this phone." This isn't a feature line; it's a description of what doesn't happen.

**Empty states**: "No entries this month." Plain.

**Errors**: clear. "That amount didn't parse. Try `12.50` or `12`."

**Sample copy**:
- Title bar: `Ledger`
- Add action: `Log spending` / `Log income`
- Export action: `Export CSV`
- Monthly view header: `April 2026` (just the month — no "Your spending in April")
- Privacy note: `Ledger has no servers. Numbers stay on this phone. CSV export is the product.`
